Plan a memorable trip — practical tips and inspiration

Welcome. This site collects what I've learned traveling South Africa: striking landscapes, local food, safety tips, and suggested routes. Use the quick links to watch vlogs, read background on culture and history, and explore Cape Town highlights.

Why visit?

South Africa combines dramatic scenery — from Table Mountain to wild savanna — with a rich cultural tapestry and world-class food and wine. It’s ideal for nature lovers, city explorers, and culture-minded travellers.

Quick essentials

  • Currency: ZAR (Rand). Carry small cash for markets.
  • Transport: rent a car for freedom; use registered shuttles in cities.
  • Health: bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and basic meds.
  • Timing: spring/autumn (Sept–Nov, Mar–May) = pleasant weather and fewer crowds.

Suggested itineraries

Short trip (5–7 days): Cape Town highlights — Table Mountain, Cape Point, Kirstenbosch and a day in the Winelands.

Two-week trip: Add Garden Route stops, charming towns (Hermanus, Knysna), and a Kruger safari for wildlife.

Food & culture

Try braai (barbecue), bobotie, biltong and a local wine pairing. Engage respectfully — ask before photographing people and learn a few greetings (e.g., "Hello" / "Sawubona").

Safety & awareness

Exercise normal travel caution: avoid showing valuables, ask locals about areas to avoid after dark, and prefer guided excursions in unfamiliar neighborhoods.
